use the Marks beyond what is described here.

## You may, without asking

- Use the name "Nirvana-OS" to refer truthfully to the project ("built with
  Nirvana-OS", "compatible with Nirvana-OS", "based on Nirvana-OS").
- Keep existing copyright, license, and attribution notices intact in copies and
  derivatives, as the SUL requires.
- Use unaltered logos solely to link back to the official project.

## You may not, without written permission

- Use the Marks, or anything confusingly similar, as the name of your fork,
  product, company, service, app, or domain.
- Use the Marks in a way that suggests endorsement, affiliation, or that your
  version is the official Nirvana-OS.
- Register or attempt to register the Marks, or confusingly similar marks, as
  trademarks, domain names, or social handles.
- Modify the logos, or use them as your own brand.

## Forks

Forks are allowed under the SUL, but a fork distributed publicly must use a
**different name and branding**. Do not present a fork as "Nirvana-OS".
